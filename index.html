The Problem

Traditional VMS Was Not Built for Regulated Environments

Every VMS on the market is built around video pipelines — RTSP streams, NVR storage, GPU farms, cloud transcoding, and retention windows measured in days. That architecture fails the industries that need analytics most.

📡

Bandwidth Chokepoint

Streaming video over satellite uplinks, cellular failover, or regulated industrial LANs is impractical or forbidden. Traditional VMS dies here.

💾

Storage Explosion

One week of 4K video storage would fill the space a full year of analytics requires. Retention becomes a hardware problem instead of a policy knob.

⚖️

Compliance Minefield

Video triggers extensive legal review in banking, cannabis, corrections, and government. Privacy regulations block deployment before it starts.

☁️

Cloud Dependency

Cloud-first VMS cannot operate in air-gapped networks, government-restricted environments, or facilities with zero outbound internet policy.

The Solution

Why ADC Wins Where VMS Cannot

ADC ingests structured metadata directly from the camera's onboard AI — not the video stream. The result is a platform that runs where nothing else can.

📡

Bandwidth — Solved

Metadata packets are tiny. ADC runs on industrial LANs, cellular failover, and satellite uplinks — anywhere streaming video would be impractical, cost-prohibitive, or outright forbidden by policy.

💾

Storage — Solved

A full year of structured event analytics fits where one week of 4K video would. 30/60/90-day retention is a configuration knob, not a hardware procurement project.

⚖️

Compliance — Solved

No video means no privacy minefield. Banks, schools, cannabis operators, corrections facilities, and government sites deploy ADC without the legal review that video systems trigger at every step.

🔒

Air-Gap — Solved

Designed to run with zero outbound internet. Internal certificate authority, Fernet-encrypted credentials at rest, TLS everywhere, and offline license activation in the roadmap.

How It Works

Simple by Design. Powerful by Architecture.

ADC sits between your existing AI cameras and your operations team — no new hardware, no video, no cloud.

1
📷

Your AI Camera

Your existing Hanwha, Axis, or Bosch AI-capable IP cameras detect events onboard — line crossings, occupancy, queues, heatmaps — using their built-in AI chip. No GPU server needed.

→
2
⚙️

AI Data Collect

ADC ingests structured metadata via MQTT (live streaming) and SFTP (scheduled statistical reports). Events are processed, validated, and persisted to TimescaleDB on your hardware — zero video, zero cloud.

→
3
📊

Your Secure Portal

Your team accesses real-time monitoring, occupancy analytics, alert management, heatmaps, and daily intelligence reports through a secure, role-based React portal — all running on your network.
